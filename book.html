WHAT HAPPENS WHEN THE BIGGEST TRANSFORMATION ISN'T DIGITAL — BUT PERSONAL?

The Manager's Matrix follows an IT leader navigating the gap between how organizations talk about leadership and how it actually feels day to day.

Through the lens of business fiction, it explores the tensions every leader carries: between confidence and doubt, passion and restraint, vision and reality.

We meet Björn Grosso — a capable, respected leader standing at a professional crossroads. After years of excelling as the reliable expert and fixer, he joins a new company with a different culture, different expectations, and a team he didn't choose and didn't build. From day one he steps into a room where loyalties have already formed and every eye is silently asking: "Who are you, and why should we follow you?"

The challenges he faces aren't about tasks anymore — they're about transformation. The very things that once made him successful (quick problem-solving, hands-on execution, relentless availability) have quietly become liabilities. It's not a ladder he's climbing. It's a matrix.

This is not a management textbook. This is the story no one tells you before you take the role.
